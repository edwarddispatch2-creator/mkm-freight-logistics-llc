Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions about DOT compliance and our services.

DOT compliance refers to meeting all requirements set by the Department of Transportation for commercial motor carriers. Non-compliance can result in fines, out-of-service orders, or even losing your operating authority. Staying compliant protects your business, your drivers, and the public.

A DOT number is a unique identifier issued by the FMCSA to commercial vehicles used in interstate commerce. If your vehicles meet certain weight or cargo thresholds, you are legally required to have one. MKM Freight Logistics handles the full registration process for you.

The Drug and Alcohol Clearinghouse is a federal database that records violations of the DOT drug and alcohol testing program. All employers, employees, and service agents involved in CDL-regulated transportation must register and comply. We manage your enrollment and ongoing reporting obligations.

IFTA (International Fuel Tax Agreement) reports are filed quarterly. Late filings result in penalties and interest. Our team handles all quarterly IFTA filings as part of our Starter and higher compliance packages.

Your CSA score (Compliance, Safety, Accountability) is calculated by the FMCSA based on inspections, violations, and crashes. A high score can flag your carrier for intervention or audits. MKM monitors your score and alerts you to issues before they escalate.

A DOT compliance review examines your driver files, hours-of-service records, vehicle maintenance logs, drug testing records, and more. Our Fleet Compliance package includes full audit binder preparation and real-time phone support during the audit itself.
